Engagement

Alfred Nzo District

05 June 2018 — by  Lerato Mamabolo
ENGAGEMENT DETAILS

Title: Eastern Cape COGTA – Planners Forum Meeting

Place:  Alfred Nzo District Offices, Mount Ayliff

MEETING SUMMARY

The NSPDR team was invited to give a presentation at the Planners Forum hosted by the ANDM. In attendance were representatives from COGTA, SALGA as well as the two local municipalities of Matatiele and Mbizana.

The district, which has been identified as one of the most impoverished in the province, is currently looking to get their SDF approved and adopted. The assistant manager from the district expressed his sentiments that the NSPDR system would be pivotal in supporting the reviewing of their SDF which is going to be carried out in house due to financial constraints. He also highlighted a pressing need to map existing capital projects in order to report to Treasury using such a system.

SALGA extended a hand of support to the project, reiterating the opportunity for the national department to partner with them in their various campaigns across the local municipalities of Eastern Cape.

The overall feedback received from the district municipalities was positive with questions being raised regarding the spatial data and cost implications for interested municipalities.

The team is looking forward to working with the officials during the upcoming functional testing phases.

Engagement

Umkhanyakhude District

16 May 2018 — by  Lerato Mamabolo
ENGAGEMENT DETAILS

Title: KZN Cogta – Operation MBO, Municipal Spatial Planning

Place:  Umkhanyakhude District Offices, Mkuze

MEETING SUMMARY

The NSPDR stakeholder management team embarked on their second visit to the Umkhanyakhude district municipality in an attempt to get an audience with the Jozini, Mtubatuba and uMhlabuyalingana local municipalities.

We were received warmly by our host from KZN Cogta and where given an opportunity to introduce the project to the attendees. The feedback we received was positive with comments given that considerations for municipalities with low bandwidth was a bonus so as not to exclude anyone from being able to fully utilise the system.

The chairperson, Mr V. Govender, expressed his views on the importance of initiatives such as these, and further highlighted the opportunity for the NSPDR team to join efforts with the current work being done in the district to capacitate the municipalities with SDF reviews that are underway.

The overall objectives of the team where realised as we established contact with individual planners who were eager to be part of the testing and training phases of the system.

We look forward to officially welcoming Umkhanyakhude and its local municipalities as partners of the project.

Consultative Conference

23 November 2017 — by  Jonathan Cleary
EVENT DETAILS

Title: NSPDR Ecosystem – Consultative Conference

Place:  Emperors Palace Hotel & Casino, Johannesburg

Enabling Rural Communities Through Spatial Technology

“An innovative way of making spatial data available and accessible to communities for more informed decision-making mechanisms in sustainable planning for land administration.”

KEYNOTE SPEAKERS
Clinton Heiman Deputy Director-General
Jonathan Cleary Project Manager
Lerato Mamabolo GIS Specialist
Willy Govender CEO - DataWorld
Professor Bo Xing University of Johannesburg
Mfanafuthi Gama NSPDR Project Sponsor
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The contents of this Post-Event Summary Report, relate to the consultative conference on the National Spatial Planning Data Repository (NSPDR) Ecosystem project – an official event of the Spatial Planning and Land Use Management branch, within the Department of Rural Development and Land Reform (DRDLR) – which took place at the Emperors Palace Hotel and Casino’s Conference Centre in Johannesburg, on Thursday, 23 November 2017.

The aim of the consultative conference was to provide a platform for national, provincial and regional/municipal policy-makers, administrators, practitioners and other related stakeholders to share their experiences, exchange ideas and most importantly of all, to hear about the new NSPDR Ecosystem and its wider benefits within the context of our theme “Enabling Rural Communities Through Spatial Technologies” that can further enhance their spatial planning development within the public sector.

The event was formally chaired by Mr Rajesh Makan, and officially opened by the A/DDG Mr Clinton Heiman. The conference then got underway with a selection of keynote speakers presenting on a multifaceted range of concepts. These were then followed by a panel discussion which took the form of an open and interactive Q&A session. After lunch the event then moved into its main focus point, which was the live demonstration of the NSPDR Ecosystem.

The workshop was broadly considered to be a success based on our preliminary objectives. It was attended by 114 delegates and participants, who’s combined inputs contributed to the depth of engagement. An attendance breakdown is included in this report; where some initial analysis has been undertaken on the attendance’s demographic – for example, there’s a split of 91 government officials to 23 corporate professionals and related stakeholders in the audience.

The National Spatial Planning Data Repository (NSPDR) conference gets underway at Emperors Palace Hotel and Casino in Johannesburg, on 23 November 2017.

  • The NSPDR ecosystem was setup to address challenges related to the availability of information for sustainable spatial planning.

  • This is in line with the NDP spatial planning imperatives of integrated planning approach.
  • The NDP spatial planning advocate for ensuring harmony between environmental, social and economic factors on a given geographic space.
